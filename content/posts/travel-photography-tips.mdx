### Creating Depth in Your Photos
1. **Foreground, Midground, Background** - Layer your composition
2. **Use Leading Lines** - Roads, rivers, fences guide the eye
3. **Overlap Elements** - Show objects in front of others
4. **Change Your Perspective** - Get low or find elevation
5. **Use Depth of Field** - Blur backgrounds to isolate subjects

## ğŸŒ… Mastering Light

### Golden Hour Magic
<div class="bg-orange-50 p-4 rounded-lg my-6">
  <h4 class="text-orange-800 font-semibold mb-2">ğŸŒ… Best Times for Photography</h4>
  <div class="space-y-2 text-orange-700 text-sm">
    <div><strong>Golden Hour:</strong> 1 hour after sunrise, 1 hour before sunset</div>
    <div><strong>Blue Hour:</strong> 20-30 minutes after sunset</div>
    <div><strong>Overcast Days:</strong> Perfect for portraits and close-ups</div>
    <div><strong>Harsh Midday Sun:</strong> Use for dramatic shadows and silhouettes</div>
  </div>
</div>

### Working with Difficult Light
- **Backlit Subjects**: Use fill flash or reflectors
- **Harsh Shadows**: Find open shade or use a diffuser
- **Low Light**: Increase ISO, use tripod, or embrace grain
- **Mixed Lighting**: Set white balance manually or shoot RAW

- **Auto ISO**: Let camera adjust sensitivity automatically

## ğŸ¯ Subject-Specific Tips

### Landscape Photography
<blockquote class="border-l-4 border-green-500 pl-4 italic text-gray-600 my-4">
"The best camera is the one that's with you, but the best landscape photo is the one you planned for."
</blockquote>

**Essential techniques:**
- Use a tripod for sharp images
- Focus 1/3 into the scene for maximum sharpness
- Use graduated ND filters for high contrast scenes
- Include foreground elements for scale and depth
- Check weather forecasts for dramatic skies

### Portrait Photography
- **Use wide apertures** (f/1.4-f/2.8) for background blur
- **Focus on the eyes** - they should always be sharp
- **Get permission** before photographing people
- **Use natural light** when possible
- **Capture candid moments** between posed shots

### Street Photography

    <li>Be discreet and unobtrusive</li>
    <li>Carry business cards to share photos</li>

### Architecture Photography
- **Use leading lines** to guide the eye
- **Shoot during golden hour** for warm light on buildings
- **Include people** for scale and life
- **Try different angles** - get low or find elevation
- **Use a wide-angle lens** but watch for distortion

## ğŸš¨ Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Technical Mistakes
- **Not checking camera settings** before important shots
- **Forgetting to format memory cards**
- **Not backing up photos** regularly
- **Shooting only in JPEG** instead of RAW
- **Ignoring the histogram** for exposure

### Creative Mistakes
- **Only shooting at eye level** - vary your perspective
- **Centering everything** - use composition rules
- **Ignoring the background** - it's part of your story
- **Over-editing photos** - keep it natural
- **Not taking enough photos** - digital is cheap!
