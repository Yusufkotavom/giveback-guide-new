## üéØ Project Overview

The Borneo Reforestation Project is a critical conservation initiative aimed at restoring degraded rainforest areas that serve as vital habitat for endangered Bornean orangutans and countless other species. Through sustainable reforestation practices and community engagement, this project addresses both environmental conservation and local economic development.

### Key Objectives
- **Habitat Restoration**: Replant native tree species in deforested areas
- **Wildlife Protection**: Create corridors connecting fragmented forest patches
- **Community Empowerment**: Provide sustainable livelihoods for local communities
- **Education**: Raise awareness about rainforest conservation
- **Research**: Study forest regeneration and wildlife recovery patterns

## üå± Conservation Methods

### Native Species Reforestation
The project focuses on planting indigenous tree species that provide:
- **Food sources** for orangutans and other wildlife
- **Nesting sites** for birds and arboreal species
- **Soil stabilization** to prevent erosion
- **Carbon sequestration** to combat climate change

### Orangutan Protection
<div class="bg-orange-50 p-4 rounded-lg border-l-4 border-orange-500 my-6">
  <h4 class="text-orange-800 font-semibold mb-2">ü¶ß Orangutan Facts</h4>
  <ul class="text-orange-700 space-y-1 text-sm">
    <li>Bornean orangutans are critically endangered</li>
    <li>They share 97% of their DNA with humans</li>
    <li>Each orangutan needs 300-400 hectares of forest</li>
    <li>Mothers care for babies for 7-8 years</li>

### Other Protected Species
The reforestation efforts also benefit:
- **Proboscis monkeys** - Endemic to Borneo
- **Sun bears** - World's smallest bear species  
- **Clouded leopards** - Elusive forest predators
- **Hornbills** - Important seed dispersers
- **Flying squirrels** - Nocturnal gliders
